01 — The Problem

Every multi-party deal dies the same three deaths.

Every system you rely on was built for yesterday's transaction flow. Loyalty engines, POS platforms, payment processors, acquirers, ERPs — they all sit inside the deal, trying to keep pace. Each one is extraordinary at what it does. Each one is a fragment of an ecosystem that has never been seen whole.

Pattern 1
The Clutch

"The accelerator's on. The clutch is in. For months." Intent exists but no mechanism connects it to action across parties.

Pattern 2
RACI Cascade

"We bounce down all the roles with no context of the whys." Context stripped at every handoff. Everyone becomes an accidental blocker.

Pattern 3
Silent No

"If I don't know, I'll just say nothing." Uncertainty causes inaction disguised as agreement. The deal dies from the absence of resolution.

Root cause: No participant can be the conductor. Every participant is inside the value chain — they have their own economics, their own perspective. By design, no single participant can be Switzerland. The value that lives between them — in the seams, in the dependencies, in the multi-party deals that none of them individually can structure — has never had a home.
